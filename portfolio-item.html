A Strange Attractor


It's like a thumbprint.


A reminder that there's order in even the most chaotic and frenzied situations, if you know how to look for it.

When we look at the world, we see chaos. But in reality, everything is interacting and influencing everything else around it. And the smallest change can have major ramifications.

A good example of that is Chaos Theory — the scientific field that deals with the seemingly random patterns found in complex systems like weather or galaxy formations.

And it turns out, there's even a mathematical concept that explains how those seemingly unconnected systems work together: strange attractors.

Strange attractors are like a fingerprint or a ghost, reminding us that no matter how chaotic a situation may seem, there is always an underlying order to be found if you know where to look for it.
